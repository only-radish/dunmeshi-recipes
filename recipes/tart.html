Man-eating Plant Tart

人喰い植物のタルト

Description

An unconventional yet beautiful tart made with various species of man-eating plants on the second floor of the dungeon. Made without any flour or eggs, this savoury yet sweet dish has really takes advantage of each unique taste of different man-eating plant fruits.

Ingredients

serves 3-4

  • 200ml Leftover scorpion broth
  • 80g slime gelatin
  • 3 medium baraselia fruit
  • 5-6 medium mearauk fruit
  • 3 medium betahn fruit
  • Salt & pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Make the crust.

    2. Lightly steam all fruit. Remove the stem and core of the baraselia by cutting a circle under the stalk and twisting and pulling the seeds out. Hit the skin of the fruit until tender, and line a cast iron pan with them to create the crust.

  2. Prepare the fruit.

    3. Roughly chop the mearauk and betahn fruit. Set some aside for garnish.

  3. Make the filling.

    4. Mash up the baraselia fruit, adding slime gelatin and some of the scorpion broth. Stir until thick and smooth. Once smooth, add the rest of the broth and chopped fruit. Mix to combine.

  4. Bake the tart.

    5. Pour the mixture into the lined cast iron pan. Let heat until the surface begins to bubble. Garnish with remaining fruit, and enjoy!
